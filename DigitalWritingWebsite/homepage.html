Why am I even here?

I WAS IN YOUR SHOES ONCE!

If you don’t know me, hi! My name is Tyler Oldano. I was an MMJ at CitrusTV for most of my college life. This photo is actually from one of my first packages I ever made for the station (This is your reminder to fix your tie before going on camera). Needless to say, it was bad. It had terrible writing, shooting, and editing. Citrus is a great place to practice making content, but because of how busy everyone is, it can be difficult to give meaningful feedback.

More often than not, a reporter will air a package and receive one to two comments about it during our post-show meeting. All the anchors and producers have to go off is the final product. We rarely get to see the actual process of making the story itself and the problems that arose. To put it simply, we’ll tell you what to improve on, but not how to do it (with some exceptions). There were times I got home after turning in my 5th mid-quality package in a row and wondered, “How am I going to get better?”

It’s time to fix that.

I’ve spent multiple semesters as a day reporter and want to give the next generation the tools I wish I had. I’m going to walk you through the process of making a news story for CitrusTV. I’ll show you what I wish I knew when I was getting my feet wet.

If you’re ready to learn (and I mean really ready), click the button below.
