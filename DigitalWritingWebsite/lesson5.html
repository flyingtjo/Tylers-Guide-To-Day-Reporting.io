Lesson Five

Research and Development

Presenting Your Work To An Audience

This final lesson focuses on what happens after your story is finished—getting your content on air and delivering it effectively as a reporter. It emphasizes that exporting your package isn’t the end of the process, but the beginning of presenting your work to an audience, whether in-studio or live in the field.

The video breaks down the fundamentals of “fronting” a story, starting with in-studio delivery at the wall. It explains how to prepare supporting visuals (VO), write anchor intros, and craft your own lead-in and closing tag. Viewers learn how to create smooth transitions into their package, avoid repetition, and use their on-camera presence to add clarity and engagement. The importance of strong tags is also highlighted, especially as a way to give the audience a sense of closure and direction on what comes next.

From there, the lesson explores live reporting—one of the most challenging but rewarding aspects of broadcast journalism. It offers practical strategies for handling live shots without a script, including using bullet points instead of memorization, thinking on your feet, and adapting to unexpected moments. It also introduces techniques like incorporating VO or graphics during a live shot and using movement or interaction to make reports more dynamic and visually engaging.

Throughout, the video reinforces the importance of communication with producers and understanding the structure of a broadcast. It encourages reporters to think beyond the package itself and focus on how their work fits into the larger show.

By the end of the lesson, viewers will have a clear understanding of how to present their stories professionally—whether at the studio wall or live on location—while building confidence, flexibility, and a stronger on-air presence.
