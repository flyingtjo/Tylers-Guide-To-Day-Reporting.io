Lesson Five

Putting Content On Air

Presenting Your Work to an Audience

Brandon Myers · May 1

This final lesson focuses on what happens after your story is finished—getting your content on air and delivering it effectively as a reporter. Exporting your package is only the beginning of presenting your work to an audience.

Fronting a Story

The lesson begins with in-studio delivery at the wall, explaining how to prepare VO (video over) elements, write anchor intros, and craft lead-ins and tags that smoothly introduce and close your story.

Strong writing and on-camera presence help avoid repetition and keep the audience engaged while maintaining clarity.

Writing for Broadcast

You’ll learn how to structure your presentation so it flows naturally within a newscast, ensuring your package fits seamlessly into the larger show.

Live Reporting

The lesson then shifts to live reporting—one of the most challenging parts of journalism. Instead of memorizing scripts, reporters use bullet points, adaptability, and clear thinking under pressure.

You’ll also explore how to incorporate VO, graphics, movement, and interaction to make live shots more dynamic and engaging.

Working with the Control Room

Communication with producers is essential. Understanding how your work fits into the broadcast structure helps ensure smooth timing and stronger storytelling.

Big Picture

The goal is to go beyond simply delivering a package—you are presenting a story to a live audience with confidence, flexibility, and professionalism.

🎤 By the end of this lesson, you’ll be able to present your reporting clearly in-studio or live in the field while building a stronger on-air presence.
