Lesson Three

In the Field

Shooting a Story

May 1

This lesson walks aspiring reporters through the real-world process of shooting a compelling news story—from preparation to capturing the final shot. It emphasizes that successful storytelling begins long before the camera is turned on, highlighting the importance of planning logistics, packing essential gear, and anticipating the needs of a shoot.

Preparation Matters

You’ll learn how to plan ahead, organize your equipment, and think through the logistics of your shoot so you’re ready for anything in the field.

What to Bring

Viewers are guided through what to include in a field kit—from must-haves like cameras, batteries, and SD cards to optional items like laptops, extra clothing, and first aid supplies—each with practical pros and cons.

The lesson also explains how to choose the right equipment depending on the story, with an emphasis on mobility and efficiency.

Core Elements of a Story

Once on location, the lesson breaks down the three essential elements of a news package: interviews, b-roll, and natural sound.

You’ll learn how to conduct both inactive and active interviews, capture dynamic and varied footage using sequences, and gather high-quality audio that enhances storytelling.

Field Techniques

Along the way, you’ll get practical tips on framing, lighting, audio monitoring, and working under tight deadlines. The lesson also covers identifying strong characters, thinking visually, and deciding whether a standup fits your story.

Big Picture

The key takeaway: gathering the right elements in the field gives you flexibility to craft a stronger, more engaging story during editing.

🎥 By the end of the lesson, you’ll know how to approach a shoot with confidence, adapt to changing situations, and capture everything you need for an effective news package.
