Lesson Three

In The Field

Shooting a Story

This lesson walks aspiring reporters through the real-world process of shooting a compelling news story—from preparation to capturing the final shot. It emphasizes that successful storytelling begins long before the camera is turned on, highlighting the importance of planning logistics, packing essential gear, and anticipating the needs of a shoot.

You’ll get practical advice on what makes a pitch stand out, how to think about feasibility and timing, and why having backup ideas is essential. We also highlight common mistakes to avoid, like conflicts of interest, unrealistic expectations, and duplicate coverage.

Viewers are guided through what to bring in their field kit, from must-haves like cameras, batteries, and SD cards to optional items like laptops, extra clothing, and first aid supplies—each with practical pros and cons. The video also introduces how to choose the right equipment for different story types, stressing mobility and efficiency.

Once on location, the lesson breaks down the three core elements of any news package: interviews, b-roll, and natural sound. It explains how to conduct both inactive and active interviews, capture dynamic and varied footage using sequences, and gather high-quality audio that enhances storytelling. Along the way, it offers practical tips on framing, lighting, audio monitoring, and working under tight time constraints.

The video also touches on identifying strong characters, thinking creatively about visuals, and deciding whether a standup is appropriate for your story. Throughout, it reinforces a key idea: gathering the right elements in the field gives you the flexibility to craft a stronger, more engaging story in the editing process.

By the end of the lesson, viewers will understand how to approach a shoot with confidence, adapt to changing situations, and capture the building blocks of an effective news package.
