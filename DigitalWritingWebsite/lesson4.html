Lesson Four

Writing and Editing

Post Production

May 1

This lesson dives into the creative and technical process of writing and editing a news package. While there’s no single “correct” way to do it, there are proven strategies that make the process smoother and more effective.

Building the Story

Think of the process like assembling a puzzle without a reference image. You’ll organize interviews, b-roll, natural sound, and standups into a clear and engaging narrative that guides the viewer from beginning to end.

Core Questions

Every story should answer the essential questions—who, what, where, when, why, and how—while also addressing the most important one: why should the audience care?

Writing for Broadcast

The lesson walks through how to structure your script, build a natural flow, and write in a concise, conversational style that connects ideas and keeps viewers engaged.

Audio & Voiceovers

You’ll learn techniques for recording high-quality voiceovers, using pacing, tone, and emphasis to enhance meaning and impact, as well as timing your script to fit standard broadcast lengths.

Editing Techniques

The editing portion reinforces sequencing shots, avoiding jump cuts, balancing audio, and using b-roll and cutaways effectively to strengthen your story.

Attention to Detail

You’ll also focus on refining your work—checking for technical errors, maintaining proper clip length, ensuring smooth transitions, and incorporating creative elements like graphics and visual storytelling.

🎬 By the end of the lesson, you’ll know how to transform raw footage into a polished, cohesive news package that is both technically strong and meaningful to your audience.
